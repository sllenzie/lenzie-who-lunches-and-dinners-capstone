Capstone reflection

Why I Built This Project

Dining recommendations are often shared casually, but they carry so much detail: the atmosphere, the context, the neighborhood energy, and the small menu decisions or interactions that make a meal more than just the food on the plate. I wanted to preserve that context instead of reducing each place to a rating or star system, and part of how I did this was to only include locations I had a positive enough experience at to remember it, even years later.

Originally, this was a lab in our Capstone class, to get us used to building with MapBox and to teach us how to geocode data and information based on the addresses of locations in our hometowns. I decided to take it a step further and build an initial database of over fifty restaurants in the Los Angeles area, to have a place for the answer to the question, "What's for dinner?"

When it came time to begin the Capstone Project, I felt a little stuck. I had just poured so much time and effort into something that felt it was only half-finished (at best) and now I needed to pivot? I had a meeting with the professors advising the class, and was given the green light to expand upon Lab 06. Cue the confetti!

I've been incredibly fortunate to visit a few places around the globe that I have had fabulously memorable meals at, so that was where the first expansion came. I began recalling, with the help of some old credit card statements for the names of certain places, the experiences dating as far back as I could remember. It was a very pleasant trip down memory lane, to recall all the positive experiences and reflect on how the food, atmosphere, and service made me feel at everywhere from the mom-and-pop diners to the elaborate tasting rooms.

Design reasoning

Optimization

I prioritized clarity and personality. The interface stays visual and easy to scan, while still keeping details accessible through cards, map popups, and modal views.

I also prioritized flexibility by structuring data in CSV format, which makes it straightforward to maintain and expand over time. In fact, since the initial creation of this site, I've added four more new places!

Technical focus

Demonstration

This project demonstrates front-end development, data cleaning, API-based geocoding, and thoughtful UX decisions in one cohesive workflow.

It reflects how I approach product thinking: start with a real user question, then shape the data and interface around that need.
