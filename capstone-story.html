Capstone reflection

Why I Built This Project

Lenzie Who Lunches and Dinners started as a personal list of restaurants I could confidently recommend to friends, as built in Lab 06 of my Digital Culture and Data Analytics Capstone course. I transformed that list into a useful, searchable experience that blends storytelling, data organization, and map-based exploration, but I wanted the database to be comprehensive, intiutive, and engaging.

The motivation

Food Is Memory, Community, and Place

Dining recommendations are often shared casually, but they carry so much detail: the atmosphere, the context, the neighborhood energy, and the small menu decisions or interactions that make a meal more than just the food on the plate. I wanted to preserve that context instead of reducing each place to a rating or star system, and part of how I did this was to only include locations I had a positive enough experience at to remember it, even years later.

This capstone gave me the opportunity to combine personal curation with technical implementation. Rather than build a generic restaurant app, which are already a dime a dozen, I focused on a specific voice and point of view: where I would actually send someone and why.

The result is intentionally human. It includes subjective notes, practical filters, and a map view that helps people plan by area, not just category, but keeps in mind the things that I would care about, such as attire.

There's people who eat to live, and there's people who live to eat. I confidently fall into the latter category, and I hope that translates through the user experience of this site.

Design reasoning

Optimization

I prioritized clarity and personality. The interface stays visual and easy to scan, while still keeping details accessible through cards, map popups, and modal views.

I also prioritized flexibility by structuring data in CSV format, which makes it straightforward to maintain and expand over time. In fact, since the initial creation of this site, I've added four more new places!

Technical focus

Demonstration

This project demonstrates front-end development, data cleaning, API-based geocoding, and thoughtful UX decisions in one cohesive workflow.

It reflects how I approach product thinking: start with a real user question, then shape the data and interface around that need.
